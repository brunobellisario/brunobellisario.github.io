Computational Data Analysis in Ecology

With big-data come great responsibilities.
The biggest challenge in modern ecology is the need to synthesize the whole range of information provided by the big-data revolution driven by technological advances. I use computational tools to integrate different types of data to understand patterns and mechanisms in community assembly processes.

Bruno Bellisario, PhD
Assistant Researcher,
Department of Agriculture and Forest Sciences (DAFNE), Tuscia University of Viterbo, Italy.

I am a community ecologist with a strong interest in data analysis and computational ecology. I use and develop tools and methods rooted in network theory to understand how different layers of information could contribute to the observed pattern of diversity.
I believe in a modern, open ecological science based on cooperation and the free use of data and methods.
